Ravital Solomon

I'm a cryptographer at NuCypher.

My research is focused on privacy-preserving smart contracts. However, my primary interest is lattice-based cryptography, a type of post-quantum cryptography.

Previously, I was part of the 11th London cohort of Entrepreneur First. I completed my MSc at Oxford in mathematics and theoretical computer science; I wrote my dissertation on lattice-based zero-knowledge proofs, focusing on their application to constructing an anonymous reputation system. My undergrad was in pure mathematics at Berkeley, where I was fortunate enough to be introduced to lattices by Ken Ribet.

I'm also very interested in education. I'm currently volunteering as a mentor at Minds Matter SF. During my time at Berkeley, I was a math tutor at the Student Learning Center (SLC). I've also worked at SY Academy (a Berkeley affiliate) as a math teacher/tutor.
